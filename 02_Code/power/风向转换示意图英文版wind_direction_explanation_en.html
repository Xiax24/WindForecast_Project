üîç Why This Transformation?

Problem: Wind direction is circular data. 0¬∞ and 360¬∞ represent the same direction (North), but numerically they appear very different. Direct correlation analysis would produce incorrect results.

Solution: Convert meteorological angles to Cartesian coordinates:

Mathematical Angle = (90¬∞ - Meteorological Angle + 360¬∞) % 360¬∞
cos(Mathematical Angle) ‚Üí X-component ‚Üí East-West (positive = East, negative = West)
sin(Mathematical Angle) ‚Üí Y-component ‚Üí North-South (positive = North, negative = South)

Meteorological North (‚Üë) East (‚Üí) Clockwise Weather, Aviation
Mathematical East (‚Üí) North (‚Üë) Counter-clockwise Trigonometry, Physics

üìê Typical Wind Direction Examples

North Wind (0¬∞)

cos(90¬∞) = 0.0 (No E-W)

sin(90¬∞) = 1.0 (North)

East Wind (90¬∞)

cos(0¬∞) = 1.0 (East)

sin(0¬∞) = 0.0 (No N-S)

South Wind (180¬∞)

cos(270¬∞) = 0.0 (No E-W)

sin(270¬∞) = -1.0 (South)

West Wind (270¬∞)

cos(180¬∞) = -1.0 (West)

sin(180¬∞) = 0.0 (No N-S)

üîÑ Practical Example: The Boundary Problem

Original wind directions: [358¬∞, 359¬∞, 0¬∞, 1¬∞, 2¬∞] (all near North)

‚ùå Direct Analysis Problem:
Max = 359¬∞, Min = 0¬∞ ‚Üí Appears to have large variation!

‚úÖ After Transformation:
cos components: [0.999, 0.999, 1.0, 0.999, 0.999] ‚Üí Very similar!
sin components: [-0.035, -0.017, 0.0, 0.017, 0.035] ‚Üí Also similar!

üí° Advantages for Correlation Analysis

Benefits of this transformation:

  • Continuity: Eliminates the 0¬∞/360¬∞ boundary problem
  • Linear Correlation: Enables standard Pearson/Spearman correlation
  • Physical Meaning: Separate East-West and North-South wind components
  • Standardized Range: Both sin and cos values are within [-1,1]
  • Meteorological Insight: Can identify which directional component affects power generation more

üè≠ Wind Power Application

In wind power forecasting:

  • East-West Component (cos): May affect turbine lateral forces and yaw system
  • North-South Component (sin): May correlate with primary power generation efficiency
  • Separate Analysis: Identifies which directional component has stronger correlation with power output
  • Terrain Effects: Different directional components may interact differently with local topography
